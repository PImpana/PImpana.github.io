The week's task was to recreate a chart that was poorly visualised.

In the US, Terry Schiavo was a popular medical case. He was removed from life support after a years-long court battle. CNN, the news channel used the chart on the left to show who agreed with the decision to remove the feeding tube. The original visualisation manipulated the chart exaggerating the difference between the support between political party supporters. It was an attempt to make the Democrats look like they supported the decision three times more than the Republicans. A closer look of the chart reveals that the difference is much smaller (62% vs 54%).

Correcting for this, the chart has been recreated on the right with accurate proportions.The original color theme has been retained while the grid has been removed.The Title has been refined and the y-axis title has been added.

Section 8:CC8. Big Data. Extracting a story from millions of prices.

The week's task was to create two charts using either of the two UK prices datasets provided.I used the dataset on AutoCPI from Davies and McElvoy (2024).

The following chart displays the mean and median prices for 6 stores for Bottled premium lager beer. The aim of presenting this chart is to visually represent the difference in average price and median price for premium lager across stores. In order to present the chart, I narrowed down information on premium lager beer through the CPI ID of the product. Then, I filtered the data by store ID and then for each store ID aggregated data for median and mean price.

This chart displays the median price trend of Premium lager beer for Store 2 as it has the highest average price from chart 1. To produce this chart, I filtered the product through CPI ID and then filtered for store 1 data. I converted the data on month and year of median prices into a readable date format. Then I created a line chart of the median price trend. This is to see how the prices evolved throughout the year.
